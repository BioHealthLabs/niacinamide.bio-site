Niacinamide Guide – How It Works & How to Use It

Niacinamide (vitamin B3) has become a staple ingredient in modern skincare for a reason. It touches several key processes in the skin at once – oil production, barrier strength, redness and uneven tone.

What Niacinamide Actually Does

  • Regulates sebum: can gradually reduce excessive oiliness.
  • Supports the barrier: helps increase ceramide and fatty acid levels.
  • Calms redness: has documented anti-inflammatory effects.
  • Improves tone: can help fade certain types of hyperpigmentation.

Choosing the Right Percentage

  • 2–5%: gentle, good for sensitive or rosacea-prone skin.
  • 5–10%: common “sweet spot” for most people.
  • 15–20%: booster territory; often better as an occasional, targeted step.

Where Niacinamide Fits in Your Routine

  1. Cleanse with a mild, low-foam cleanser.
  2. Optional mist or hydrating toner.
  3. Niacinamide serum or lotion.
  4. Moisturiser if needed.
  5. SPF 30+ every morning.

Already overwhelmed by actives? You may not need another separate serum – a moisturiser containing niacinamide can be enough.

Good Niacinamide “Neighbours”

  • Gentle hydrating toners and serums.
  • Ceramide-rich moisturisers.
  • Daily sunscreen.
  • Retinoids and acids, when used sensibly in the same routine.

Niacinamide is flexible – it works in both morning and evening routines. Choose the slot that feels easiest to stick with consistently.
